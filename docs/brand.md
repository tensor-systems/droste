# Droste brand

Decided 2026-07-06. Brutalist: the mark and the type say what the tool is —
raw, exact, terminal-native.

## The mark

Self-similar square frames, each rotated **16°** and scaled **×0.70** into
the last — the *Print Gallery* transformation (an image containing itself,
turning as it shrinks) — converging on a **filled block rotated 64°**: the
next frame in the sequence, solid. Escher left the center of Print Gallery
blank; the center is what the recursion computes. **The block is the answer.**

Geometry (viewBox −50…50): frame half-sizes 40 · 28 · 19.6 · 13.7, strokes
6.4 · 5.6 · 5.0 · 4.4 (taper ×0.88), hard corners (`rx=0`). Core: 13.2-unit
square at 64°. Small cuts drop to 2–3 frames with heavier strokes (see the
favicon cuts in the identity sheet).

## Palette

Escher printed black on white; color only ever flat and structural.

| role | light | dark |
|---|---|---|
| paper | `#F3F2EE` | `#141414` |
| ink | `#131313` | `#EBEAE5` |
| print blue (the answer block) | `#1F3CC4` | `#4A63E8` |

No other colors. No gradients, no shadows, no rounded corners. Blue-on-paper
is blueprint/cyanotype heritage and the raw web's link color — and unowned
in the AI space. House family note: Cozy is warm (the room at night), Droste
is cool (the print on the wall); in both, a single accent element is *the
answer* — Cozy's curl holds it, Droste's recursion arrives at it.

## Type

Wordmark: grotesque black caps (system heavy sans), tight tracking.
Everything else: monospace, letter-spaced caps for labels. No italics.
